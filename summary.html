Plot Overview

Crime and Punishment by Fyodor Dostoevsky is a novel written in the 1860s and set in what was then present-day Russia. The novel follow the story of a young, idealistic an, Rodion Raskolnikov, who commits a murder in the hopes of improving society. This idea that certain individuals have the right to commit evil for the overall betterment of society is a recurring theme throughout the plot. As the story progresses, Raskolnikov meets various different characters, including the detective Porfiry, the chaste prostitute Sonya, and the evil yet generous Svidrigalov, all the while becoming more insecure and guilty about the crime he committed. Ultimately, Raskolnikov confesses to his crime and the story reaches a definitive resolution in which honesty, duty, and practicality triumph over evil performed with idealistic intent.
